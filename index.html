Information

Kcal
Kilocalories are units of energy. In nutrition they quantify the energy that a food or drink provides, or the energy burned during exercise.
Macronutrients
Macronutrients are essential nutrients that the body needs in large amounts to remain healthy. The 3 main macronutrients are Proteins, Fat, and Carbohydrates.
Recommended daily amounts of each:
  • Proteins: 1.6 grams per body kg
  • Fat: 0.8 grams per body kg
  • Carbohydrates: the rest of your Kcal intake
    • BMR
    Basal Metabolic Rate measures the amount of Kcal required to maintain vital body functions while at rest.
    TDEE
    Total Daily Energy Expenditure is calculated taking the BMR multiplied by an Activity Level, and it's an estimated number of total Kcal you need to maintain your body weight.
    Convert to imperial units
  • To convert weight: kilograms = pounds / 2.2
  • To convert height: centimeters = inches x 2.54
    • About losing fat
    To lose fat you need to be in a calorie deficit, meaning that you consume less Kcal than you burn. A good starting point can be to reduce your daily Kcal intake by 300 Kcal.
    About building muscle
    Besides regular exercise, the optimal way to build muscle is to be in a calorie surplus, meaning that you consume more calories than you burn. A good starting point can be to increase your daily Kcal intake by 200 Kcal.
    About beginners
    At beginner or even intermediate level, you can build muscle while being in a calorie deficit, the muscle gain may be slower but it's possible, so if your goal is just to lose fat and be fit it's a good option !
